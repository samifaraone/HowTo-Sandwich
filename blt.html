    INGREDIENTS


    4 slices bacon
    2 slices white bread
    2 leaves iceberg lettuce, fresh and full
    1 slice of ripened tomatoes
    2 tablespoons mayonnaise



    DIRECTIONS


    Cook bacon until crispy, then drain on paper towels.
    Toast the 2 slices of bread.
    Spread 1 tablespoon mayo on each slice of toasted bread. (More or less, to taste).
    Add 1 slice of lettuce to 1 pieces of mayo-spreaded toast.
    Add 1 slices of tomato on top of lettuce.
    Arrange 4 slices of bacon evenly on top of tomato. (Break bacon slices in half to fit, if needed)
    Put the remaining pieces of mayo-spreaded toast on top to finish the sandwiches.
    Enjoy!

    Fun facts


    BLT (Bacon, Lettuce & Tomato)

    The abreviation 'BLT' first appeared in print during the early 1950s, but had already entered into common usage during the late 1940s.

    The BLT is still the second-most popular sandwich in the United States (the simple ham sandwich is first).

    The BLT, (Bacon, Lettuce and Tomato sandwich) became popular when fresh lettuce and tomatoes became available year round with the rapid expansion of supermarkets after World War II.

    A BLT is best when made at home, rather than ordered at a restaurant.
